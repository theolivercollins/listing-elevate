> **The short version:** Upload → Direct → Deliver. Three steps, one day.

You don't have to understand any of this to use Listing Elevate — but if you're
curious what happens between hitting **submit** and getting your video, here it
is in plain language.

## 1. Upload

You drop in 20–60 photos and your listing details. Behind the scenes we tidy up
every photo — exposure, orientation, and framing — so the source material is
consistent before anything is built from it.

## 2. Direct

This is where the "AI" does its work. The system looks at each photo and decides
how to shoot it — the same calls a human videographer makes:

- **What's in the room** — kitchen, primary bedroom, pool, exterior, and so on.
- **How the camera should move** — a slow push toward a feature, an orbit around
  a kitchen island, a drone-style pull-back on the exterior, a reveal through a
  doorway. Each shot is matched to what the photo can actually support, so the
  motion looks natural rather than forced.
- **The order of the shots** — a walkthrough that flows the way a buyer would
  actually move through the home.
- **Voice and mood** — if you added a voiceover, a script is written to match the
  property and the tone you asked for.

From this plan, each shot is generated as a short cinematic clip and the clips
are stitched into a single edited video, with music and any voiceover.

## 3. Deliver

Before anything reaches you, a **human editor reviews the cut**. This is the step
that keeps quality consistent — a person checks the result looks right, fixes
anything off, and signs off.

Then you receive your finished video in the formats you ordered (16:9 for the MLS,
9:16 for social, or both), ready to download, share, or embed.

## How long does it take?

**Under 24 hours** from the moment you finish uploading — every time. Most
orders come back well inside that window. You'll be notified the moment your
video is ready; there's nothing to watch or refresh.

## What you don't have to do

- No scheduling a shoot.
- No crew or equipment.
- No editing software.
- No back-and-forth to get the camera work right — the direction is handled, and
  you only step in if you want to request a change.
