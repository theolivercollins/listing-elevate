# Photo guidelines

Last updated: 2026-06-14

Your video is only as good as the photos it's built from. The good news: the bar
is low. Phone photos work. Here's how to get the best result.

## The essentials

| | |
|---|---|
| **How many** | 20–60 photos. Minimum 10. More gives the director more to work with. |
| **Quality** | Standard listing or phone-camera quality is fine. Higher resolution is better but not required. |
| **Orientation** | Mix portrait and landscape freely — we handle it. |
| **What we fix for you** | Exposure, cropping, framing, and the order shots appear in. |

## What makes a great set

- **Cover the whole home.** Include every key space: kitchen, living areas,
  primary bedroom, bathrooms, other bedrooms, and outdoor spaces.
- **Lead with your best rooms.** The kitchen, primary suite, and standout
  features (pool, view, lanai) carry the most weight — make sure they're
  well represented with a few angles each.
- **Get the exterior.** Front exterior and any aerial/drone shots make strong
  opening and closing moments.
- **Show features clearly.** If a room has a fireplace, island, or a view worth
  selling, include a photo where it's clearly visible — the director can build a
  shot that highlights it.

## What to avoid

- **Blurry or very dark photos.** We correct exposure, but we can't rescue a shot
  that's badly out of focus.
- **Heavy filters or watermarks.** Send the cleanest version you have.
- **Duplicates of the same angle.** A little variety per room beats ten near-
  identical shots.
- **Photos with people or pets** in frame, unless you specifically want them.

## A note on motion

The system chooses camera moves based on what each photo can actually support.
A flat, head-on photo of a small bathroom will get a gentle, appropriate move; a
deep hallway or a wide exterior can support something more dramatic. You don't
need to plan any of this — just give a good spread of rooms and angles and the
direction takes care of itself.

If a delivered video doesn't feature a room the way you'd like, that's exactly
what a [revision](./05-reviewing-and-revisions.md) is for.
