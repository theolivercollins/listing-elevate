This page gets you from zero to your first listing video.

## 1. Create your account

1. Go to <https://listingelevate.com>.
2. Click **Start a video** (or **Sign in** if you already have an account).
3. Enter your email. We send a **magic link** — no password to remember.
   Click the link in your inbox and you're signed in.

That's the whole sign-up. There's no approval queue and no credit card required
to look around.

> **Tip:** Magic links expire after a short window. If yours doesn't work,
> request a fresh one — don't reuse an old email.

## 2. Find your way around the dashboard

After signing in you land on your **dashboard**. The main areas:

| Area | What it's for |
|---|---|
| **Home / Overview** | A snapshot of your in-progress and recent videos |
| **Properties** | Every listing you've submitted, with status and delivered cuts |
| **Upload / Start a video** | The order flow — create a new video |
| **Videos library** | Your finished videos, organized into folders |
| **Account / Settings** | Your details, brand kit, and preferences |

What you see depends on your role. Standard agent accounts see their own
properties and videos. Brokerage admins see additional team controls.

## 3. Place your first order

Click **Start a video** and follow the four steps:

1. **Style** — pick the video type, length, and format.
2. **Property** — enter the address and listing details.
3. **Add-ons** — optional voiceover and any custom requests.
4. **Photos** — upload 20–60 photos.

Each step is covered in detail in [Creating a video](./02-creating-a-video.md).

When you submit, the clock starts. You'll get your finished video back in **under
24 hours**, and you can close the tab — everything runs in the background and
you'll be notified when it's ready.

## 4. While you wait

Your new listing shows up under **Properties** with a live status. You don't
need to do anything until it's ready to review — see
[How it works](./03-how-it-works.md) for what's happening behind the scenes, and
[Reviewing & revisions](./05-reviewing-and-revisions.md) for what to do when the
cut lands.
